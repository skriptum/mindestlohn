Sonneberg

Sonneberg: - Germany’s first AfD district administrator (Landrat) - Robert Sesselmann - prevails in run-off election against conservative CDU - 52.8% to 47.2%

shocking to political establishment, international coverage

Sonneberg also: district with the highest proportion of minimum wage earners in DE - 2022 increase in minimum wage to 12 euros - 44% of Sonneberg residents benefited from this - the highest in Germany

Research Question: What is the connection between minimum wage and populism?

  • thüringenweit höchste Industriedichte
  • schon damals viele Arbeiter, die erst rot und dann NS gewählt haben (Wiki)

Method

Linear Regressio with OLS Estimator

\[ \text{afd}_k = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \times \text{min}_k + \epsilon \]

  • \(\text{afd}_k\) AfD Vote Share in Landkreis \(k\)
  • \(\text{min}_k\) Share of Minimum Wage Recipients in Landkreis \(k\)
  • \(\beta_0\) Intercept
  • \(\beta_1\) Slope
  • \(\epsilon\) Error

Theory

Populism = multicausal, not only socioeconomic, but also sociodemographic, cultural, …

i want to explain where MW fits exisitng research

status disordance (Kurer and Staalduinen 2022)

  • connected to eastern germany & minimum wage
  • MW = lower status than expected from parents with stable jobs in GDR
  • also generally disappointed by it

MW recipients = workers at lower end of spectrum

  • instability of job = insecurity (Hövermann 2023)
  • wish back good old times with stable jobs pre neoliberalism

despise for inequality (Bartels 2019)

  • lower end of wage scale (feels like down)
  • disappointed by system
  • see inequality
  • inequality => populism

lack of social mobility (Dodin et al. 2021) adn (Alesina, Stantcheva, and Teso 2018)

  • if high share of MW = not many higher paying jobs
  • esp. eastern germans = not many high earning positions
  • future pessimism = easier for populists

eastern germany (Snower and Merkl 2006)

  • hard hit by post-Wende turmoil
  • creation of Niedriglohnsektor due to TreuHand policy
  • cultural factors as well

inflation (Tober 2022)

  • hits lower wage workers harder
  • especially rural regions with high share of card dependency

why only now, not earlier?

  • Voice Exit by (Hirschman 1972)
  • before, exit (abstention) only option for voters incontempt with situation
  • with rise of AfD = voice

labor market hypothesis?

  • mostly rejected empirically
  • because rationally no risk of losing job (low unempl. share)
  • but maybe irrational fear

Afd position on MW

  • before 2015 introduction = “jobkiller” by chef petry
  • changed away from this towards a more social-nativist standpoint
  • “Grundsatzprogramm” supports MW concept

future Research

  • panel data set with more than one point in time
